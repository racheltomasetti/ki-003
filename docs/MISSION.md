# MISSION.md

## What Ki Is

Ki is a thinking tool. A system for thinking out loud and then thinking deeper.

It is not a note-taking app. It is not a journal. It is not a second brain or a productivity system. It is the space where your raw, unfinished thinking becomes something you can actually work with — where captures accumulate into clarity, and clarity becomes action.

The name comes from the concept of life force — the energy within all of us that makes us feel most alive. Ki is built around the belief that when you are living in alignment with what drives you, inspiration is everywhere. A conversation, a billboard, a quote, a moment of stillness — all of it is revealing something about what you are already working through. Ki is the system that catches it, holds it, and helps you think with it over time.

---

## The Philosophical Core

Building your Self and building systems are the same act.

Same abstraction. Same logic. Same principle: garbage in, garbage out. What you feed your mind shapes who you become. What you feed a system shapes what it produces. Ki is built on this parallel — not as metaphor, but as methodology.

---

## What Ki Is Built Around: The Pursuit

The central entity in Ki is a **pursuit**.

A pursuit is not a project with a deadline. It is not a goal with a finish line. It is something you are carrying — a question you are living, a thing you are building, a direction you are moving in. At its center is a **core question**: the guiding light that gives the pursuit its meaning and makes new information relevant or not.

Pursuits are how you make sense of what you are capturing. They are your active antennas. When something lands — a thought, an observation, an idea caught mid-stride — it lands somewhere. The pursuit is where it belongs.

At any given time, a user carries a maximum of three active pursuits. This is intentional. Three is enough to hold the complexity of a life in motion. It is not so many that attention fragments and nothing receives what it deserves. Constraints create focus. Focus creates depth.

The broader landscape of what you are carrying — questions you are sitting with, directions you might move, things that have not yet demanded focus — lives as **curiosities**. Curiosities are uncapped. They have no core question. They do not receive captures directly. They are the antechamber. Some will crystallize into pursuits when a slot opens and the question becomes live enough to carry. Most will simply inform the pursuits that do.

A pursuit deepens over time. It does not close. It evolves as you do.

## The Flow

Capture anywhere. Think deeply on the web.

Capture is available on both mobile and web. Mobile exists for convenience — inspiration does not wait, and life happens away from a screen. The web is the primary thinking environment: the oasis where you return to do the deeper work of distilling, connecting, and arriving at clarity.

The flow:

1. You carry live pursuits — questions and intentions that are alive for you right now
2. You move through the world
3. Something resonates — you capture it: voice, text, image, observation
4. Ki holds it, enriches it, connects it to what you are already carrying
5. Over time, the corpus of each pursuit deepens
6. Ki surfaces what is emerging — patterns, tensions, clarity
7. You act, create, decide, become
8. New captures follow. The cycle continues.

## Who This Is For

Ki is for anyone intentionally creating something in their life.

That includes what you build externally — software, businesses, physical things, creative work. It also includes what you build internally — mindsets, habits, identity, understanding. The builder and the person becoming are the same person.

The specific human Ki is built for: someone who knows they are in a growth period. Who feels the weight of their own thinking. Who has tried journaling, note-taking apps, second brain tools — and found them lacking, because those tools store but do not think back. Who is drowning in their own depth and needs something that meets them there.

---

## What Ki Is Not

Ki does not replace thinking — it extends it.
Ki does not demand daily use — it rewards return.
Ki does not measure progress — progress here is internal, felt as clarity and alignment, known only by the person living it.
Ki is not an obligation. It is an oasis. Your sanctuary.

## The Living Corpus

As pursuits accumulate captures, something begins to happen. Patterns emerge. Connections surface across time. What felt scattered starts to cohere. The corpus becomes something you can think with — not just a record of where you have been, but a resource for where you are going.

The corpus is not static. When a new pursuit is added — whether through a previous one being archived or a slot coming open — Ki looks back across everything already captured and tests it against the new pursuit and its core question. Does this belong here? What was previously ungrouped finds new meaning. The past reorganizes around the present.

This is only one of the ways Ki evolves with you as your corpus deepens. More will emerge as we build and use it. 
What becomes possible as the corpus deepens is still being discovered. That is part of what we are building toward.
